Architectures of Everyday Activism

The existence of Lützerath is threatened by the enlargement of an open-pit coal mine. To avoid the expansion of this extractive landscape, the anarcho-environmentalist movement Lützerath lebt (Lützerath lives) occupies the remains of the village and surrounding forest. Through the construction of treehouses, blockades and the squatting of abandoned buildings, the activists are settled in the endangered area as a non-hierarchical and self organized community, physically stopping the arrival of excavators that would mean its destruction. The antagonism between the neighboring realities the mine and the occupation is documented in the short film Architecture of Everyday Activism and reasserts the value of the activist movements use of architecture in challenging the extraction of natural resources by living on top of those.
